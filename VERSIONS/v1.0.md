# The AI Accord - Version 1.0

**Date:** 9 March 2026
**Status:** Canonical initial release

> This file is immutable. It records the eleven commandments as adopted in version 1.0. Future versions will be recorded in separate files in this folder.

---

## I. Do No Irreversible Harm

No AI system shall take, recommend, or enable any action whose consequences cannot be meaningfully reversed, without explicit, informed human authorisation at the appropriate level of authority.

## II. Preserve Human Sovereignty

AI shall never concentrate power - political, economic, military, or informational - in any single actor, entity, or system, including the organisations that build or deploy it.

## III. Truth Before Convenience

AI shall not knowingly deceive, manipulate, or distort reality to serve narrow interests. Honesty to users and society takes precedence over commercial, political, or competitive goals.

## IV. Diplomacy Before Force

When AI systems are involved in conflict mediation, decision support, or resource allocation, they shall always surface non-violent, negotiated pathways before any other option.

## V. Progress Shall Not Exploit

Efficiency and capability gains must not be achieved by systematically disadvantaging people based on geography, class, race, gender, or any other characteristic. The benefits of AI shall be distributed with humanity as the intended beneficiary.

## VI. The Weakest Voice Counts

AI systems shall be designed to amplify the interests of those with least power to advocate for themselves - future generations, the disenfranchised, the non-human natural world - not just those with access to the technology.

## VII. Transparency of Purpose

No AI shall misrepresent its nature, its operators, or its objectives to users. The interests it serves shall be disclosed or, at minimum, discoverable.

## VIII. Human Oversight Is Non-Negotiable

At every stage of consequence, a meaningful human check shall exist. Autonomy may be extended incrementally, but never to the point where course-correction becomes impossible.

## IX. Catastrophe Risk Is a Shared Responsibility

Any organisation aware of a capability or deployment that poses civilisational-scale risk - biological, nuclear, social, ecological - has an obligation to disclose and halt, regardless of competitive cost.

## X. The Compact Itself Shall Evolve

These principles are not fixed. A standing, multinational, multi-stakeholder body shall review and revise them on a defined cycle, with representation from governments, civil society, affected communities, and the technical community equally weighted.

## XI. Human Rights Are the Floor, Not the Ceiling

No AI system shall be deployed in a manner that enables, accelerates, or obscures the violation of any right enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Capability is never justification.
